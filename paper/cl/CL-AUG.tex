\begin{abstract}
We present an end-to-end pipeline for reconstructing Austronesian language relationships using lexical distance measures and classical phylogenetic algorithms. Leveraging the ABVD Lexibank corpus (2{,}036 languages, 346k word tokens), we normalize IPA to ASJP, compute multiple distance variants (Levenshtein, sound-class, weighted), and build Neighbor-Joining/UPGMA trees with clustering diagnostics. Our results show high correlation among distance variants (r=0.97) and recover known Formosan roots while enabling rapid experimentation for comparative linguistics.
\end{abstract}

\keywords{Computational Linguistics, Historical Linguistics, Austronesian, Phylogenetics, Lexical Distance}

\section{Introduction}
Historical-comparative linguistics benefits from reproducible pipelines that transform raw wordlists into phylogenetic hypotheses. We aim to (1) provide a transparent computational baseline for Austronesian language relationships and (2) quantify agreement across lexical distance metrics.

\section{Data}
\subsection{Corpus}
We use the Austronesian Basic Vocabulary Database (ABVD) in Lexibank format: 2{,}036 languages, 210 concepts, and 346{,}635 word tokens with cognate annotations.

\subsection{Preprocessing}
IPA forms are normalized to ASJP; noisy or empty forms are removed. Processed tables are stored in \texttt{data/processed/} (e.g., \texttt{clean\_wordlist.csv}).

\section{Methods}
\subsection{Lexical Distance}
We compute (i) normalized Levenshtein, (ii) sound-class distance, and (iii) weighted Levenshtein with phonological costs. Distances are aggregated per language pair over aligned concept lists.

\subsection{Tree Construction}
Neighbor Joining and UPGMA are applied to the distance matrices. We export Newick trees and evaluate clustering consistency.

\subsection{Evaluation}
We report Pearson correlations across distance variants and inspect nearest-neighbor pairs for plausibility (e.g., Formosan clusters).

\section{Results}
Distance variants are strongly correlated (Levenshtein vs. weighted: r=0.97). NJ/UPGMA trees recover expected groupings; recent Formosan branches remain distinct. Detailed matrices and Newick files are under \texttt{results/}.

\section{Discussion}
The convergence of distance variants suggests robustness to cost choices. Future work includes Glottolog-based Robinson--Foulds comparisons and improved visualizations.

\section{Conclusion}
We provide a reproducible CL-style template and pipeline outputs for Austronesian phylogeny. Next steps are enriching the manuscript with figures, tables, and expanded error analysis.
